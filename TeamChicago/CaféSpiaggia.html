Café Spiaggia

While Spiaggia remains a landmark of high-style Italian dining, you're more likely to find local shoppers and Gold Coast residents eating their lunch at its quiet little sister, Café Spiaggia, which lives down the hall and around a sharp corner. Here you'll find one of Chicago's prettiest, most cosmopolitan cafés, with windows looking out on the lake and walls covered with colorful faux-Renaissance murals. The menu doesn't think like a second-rate knockoff café. As consistent as Spiaggia itself, it turns out crisp, thin-crust pizzas and first-rate pastas (among the best: a classic spaghetti with clams and white wine, and gnocchi tossed with green beans, potatoes, and pesto).
